## Bear Case

# DAKTRONICS BEAR THESIS

• **Tariff exposure is structural and unresolved.** The company manufactures 80% of output in the US but sources components from 40+ countries, with China as a critical LED/semiconductor supplier. While management "may pursue potential refunds" through CBP's refund portal, they explicitly state "no amounts related to potential refunds have been recorded" and acknowledge timing and amounts are "uncertain." This means tariff headwinds already baked into fiscal 2026 results may persist while upside is speculative.

• **Gross margin recovery remains elusive despite pricing actions.** Gross margin improved to 27.3% in fiscal 2026 from 25.8% in fiscal 2025, but is still below historical norms. Management states tariffs "have adversely impacted gross margins" and that "while we seek to mitigate these impacts through pricing actions...we may not be able to fully offset increased costs." The filing provides no evidence that pricing power is keeping pace with input cost inflation.

• **New Mexico facility is a near-term margin drag with uncertain payoff.** The company is opening a manufacturing facility in Saltillo, Mexico in fiscal 2027. Management acknowledges "the pace of the production ramp-up and the extent and timing of any associated financial benefits depend on execution, staffing, and market conditions." This is capital deployment with delayed returns during a period when near-term profitability is already pressured.

• **Federal funding dependency amid policy uncertainty.** The filing states tariffs "influenced customer purchasing behavior, particularly for projects dependent on federal funding," yet provides no disclosure of federal funding as a percentage of revenue. With geopolitical tensions and shifting U.S. trade policy creating "delays in customer decision-making," this revenue stream is opaque and discretionary.

• **Execution risks are explicitly material to near-term profitability.** Management warns "there may be periods in which sales levels and expense trends are not fully aligned" and that ongoing investments "may exert pressure on near-term profitability." With 2,693 employees globally and complex manufacturing integration across five segments, execution missteps could easily offset the 10.9% revenue growth posted in fiscal 2026.

## The Verdict

The core tension is whether Daktronics' 150 bps gross margin recovery in FY2026 reflects durable pricing leverage and cost discipline, or a one-year benefit from strong demand that will erode if revenue growth slows or tariff/input costs accelerate further. The Mexico facility also introduces near-term drag during a period when management acknowledges profitability may be pressured by execution investments.

- **Bull is right if:** Gross margin must stabilize at or above 27% as revenue continues to grow, demonstrating that pricing actions have durably offset tariff and input cost inflation. The Mexico facility must ramp without material margin compression and begin yielding cost efficiencies within 12–18 months post-launch.
- **Bear is right if:** Either revenue growth to decelerate below 10% while tariffs remain elevated, forcing management to absorb cost inflation via margin compression, or gross margin to retreat below 26% as customer pricing resistance emerges or input costs re-accelerate. The Mexico facility must experience significant startup delays or staffing/execution challenges that delay profitability contributions.
- **Watch:** Gross margin trend in Q1–Q2 FY2027 (the first full quarters post-fiscal year-end): if it holds at or above 27%, the bull's pricing narrative gains credibility; if it slides below 26%, the bear's concern about unsustainable one-year margin recovery becomes material. Also monitor management's quantification (or lack thereof) of tariff refund recovery and any guidance revision on Mexico facility contribution timing.
